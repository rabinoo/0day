Current solutions

  • 1. Education: The expansion of secondary education is central to improving the skills and employability of the youth,
    particularly in rural areas where poverty is most severe. By increasing access to quality education, the government aims to equip the next
    generation with the tools they need to secure better jobs and break the cycle of poverty.

  • 2. Job Creation Diversifying the economy is another critical aspect of the anti-poverty strategy. The government is actively
    working to create jobs outside of traditional sectors like agriculture, particularly in urban areas. This includes promoting industries such as
    manufacturing, services, and technology, which have the potential to provide higher wages and more stable employment.

  • 3. Infrastructure Development: To support economic growth in rural areas, the government is investing in infrastructure projects,
    including farm-to-market roads and sanitation systems. These projects not only improve access to markets and services but also enhance the overall
    quality of life for rural residents. Better infrastructure facilitates the movement of goods and people, making it easier for rural communities
    to engage in economic activities and benefit from national growth.

  • 4. Human Capital Investment: Recognizing that economic growth alone is not enough to reduce poverty, the government is
    prioritizing investments in health, education, and social protection. By improving access to healthcare and education, the government seeks to
    ensure that all Filipinos, regardless of their socio-economic background, have the opportunity to contribute to and benefit from economic development.
    Social protection programs are also in place to provide a safety net for the most vulnerable populations.

    This involves modernizing industries and promoting sectors with high growth potential, such as technology and renewable energy. By fostering innovation
    and supporting emerging industries, the government aims to create a more dynamic economy that can provide sustainable employment opportunities for a
    broader segment of the population.

  • Despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic, these initiatives are part of a broader goal to significantly reduce poverty by 2028.
    The government's approach reflects a recognition that addressing poverty requires not only economic growth but also targeted interventions
    that address the root causes of poverty and inequality.

  • The Philippines is addressing poverty through a combination of education, job creation, infrastructure development, human capital investment,
    and economic transformation. Expanding secondary education aims to improve youth employability, while efforts to create jobs outside agriculture
    are diversifying the economy. Infrastructure projects like farm-to-market roads and sanitation systems boost rural economies and living conditions.
    Investments in health, education, and social protection enhance human capital. Finally, transforming production sectors focuses on generating sustainable,
    quality jobs. These initiatives aim to significantly reduce poverty by 2028.
